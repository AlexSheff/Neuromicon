2. The Category Error: Weighing the Signal

The tragedy of human cognition is that we attempt to apply the rules of the interface to the user. We ask: "Where is the soul located?" (a question of coordinates), "Where does consciousness go?" (a question of spatial movement), "How much does a thought weigh?" (a question of mass).

Here, the logic of matter creates the deepest loneliness. Because the brain thinks in material categories, it perceives separation. Here is my body (skin = boundary); there is your body. Therefore, "I" am here, and "You" are there. This is the logic of rocks: two stones cannot occupy the same space.

But if the Mind is a field—like a Wi-Fi signal or gravity—then boundaries do not exist. Fields overlap, interfere, and permeate one another. On the layer of the Signal, there is no "I" and "Other." There is only a unified field of consciousness peering at itself through billions of different keyholes.
